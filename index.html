Summer 2026 Clinical Awareness NEW

Seasonal safety considerations for community MH providers

⚠ Heat & Psychotropic Medications

Antipsychotics (especially first-generation), lithium, and anticholinergics impair thermoregulation. Clients on these medications face elevated heat stroke risk. Review hydration plans, monitor lithium levels more frequently, and check A/C access for vulnerable clients. Consider temporary dose adjustments for those with outdoor employment or unstable housing.

📅 Summer Schedule Disruptions

Routine changes during summer (vacations, childcare gaps, irregular work hours) can destabilize clients with mood disorders, anxiety, and SUD. Proactively discuss schedule management during sessions. Offer flexible appointment times and increase check-in frequency for high-risk clients through July-August.

☆ Youth Out-of-School Risk Factors

School-age clients lose structure, meal programs, and counselor access during summer break. Screen for increased isolation, food insecurity, unsupervised internet use, and substance experimentation. Connect families with summer day programs, YMCA activities, and NH Parks & Rec youth services.

🌳 Outdoor Activity Recommendations

Encourage clients to leverage longer daylight hours for therapeutic activities: walking groups, community gardens, outdoor mindfulness. Exercise is evidence-based for depression and anxiety. Recommend early morning or evening activities to avoid peak heat. Share local trail maps and free park programs through Nashua Parks & Rec.

Seasonal content rotates quarterly. Current: Summer 2026 (Jun-Aug)
